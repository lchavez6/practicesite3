Work Experience

My work experience mainly consists of customer service and hospitality. Although I’ve always wanted to teach, I was too busy throughout college to tutor or substitute so I took up various serving jobs at different restaurants. Currently, I am working at a fine dining wine and steakhouse while also doing freelance Spanish interpreting. Before this, I worked as a teller at a bank, and also as a dispatcher for the Washington Dulles Airport. When it comes to my college education, I took the general required credits for my first two years, and when I became an English major, I chose Literature as my concentration. I took various African American and Literature classes, Latino Literature Classes, Creative Writing, Literary Theory, and varying Literature classes from different time period. When I got into graduate school, I also pursued English Literature but I got to take different classes such as poetry, Native American Literature, and British Literature.

Career

My goal was to become a teacher. I wanted to teach secondary education and be an English Teacher either in Loudoun County or in Fairfax County. I wanted to get my master’s degree so I could also teach AP classes as well as dual enrollment. I would also like to teach summer school and become an adjunct part-time at a nearby community college. I think that teaching would be very fulfilling because I will get to influence future minds and give back to my community. Most recently, I accepted a position as an English teacher at a middle school nearby and I’m very excited to start my new job.

Portfolio

If I had pursued a thesis, I would have written it about Kate Chopin's The Awakening and the ways that femininity is limited within the society that the novel takes place. I have also included term papers for the classes I found most interesting. Such as early American Literature, Literary Criticism, and even Shakespeare. I found that I geared mostly towards race relations and the complications of sexuality and gender.
